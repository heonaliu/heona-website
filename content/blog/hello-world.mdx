# Hello World

Every developer has written those two words. Usually it's the first program — a simple print statement that says "I exist" to the terminal. Today, I'm saying it to the internet.

## Why This Blog?

I've always kept notes — scattered across Notion, voice memos, random text files. Ideas, reflections, things I learned, things I failed at. For a long time, I kept them private, because who would want to read them?

But I think that's the wrong question. The right question is: *what do I want to put into the world?*

And the answer is: honest, specific stories about learning to build things. About the intersection of code and creativity. About being a student who makes things, makes mistakes, and makes them again.

## What This Will Be

This blog won't be a tutorial site. There are a thousand better resources for learning React or Python. What I want to write here is messier and more personal:

- Reflections after hackathons (the good, the chaotic, the humbling)
- Thoughts on digital art and the creative process
- Project postmortems — what went right, what went sideways
- Notes on books, papers, and ideas that stuck with me
- Honest takes on learning and growing in this field

## The Tech Behind This

This site is built with **Next.js**, **Tailwind CSS**, and **Framer Motion** — with Firebase for authentication and a Markdown blog system. I built it from scratch because I wanted to understand every piece of it. And because I wanted it to feel like *me*.

The design goal was "minimal but alive" — clean, fast, and full of small delightful animations without being overwhelming.

## A Promise

I'll try to be honest here. Not performatively vulnerable (nobody needs that), but genuinely real. When something's hard, I'll say so. When I don't know something, I'll admit it.

The internet has enough polished takes. I want to add some rough edges.

---

Thanks for being here, even if it's just you, future me, rereading this.

— Heona
